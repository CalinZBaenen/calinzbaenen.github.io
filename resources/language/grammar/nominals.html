What is a nominal?

A nominal is any word that plays the role of:

  1. an identifier, such as the name Google,
  2. an object, such as the word "apple",
  3. a word that conveys ownership, such as "my", "your", and "their",
  4. a word that specifies a person, such as "I", "them", and "you".

Nominals are typically divided into two subclasses, nouns and pronouns.

What is a noun?

A noun, which may also be called a "substantive", is any word that refers to an object, abstract idea, or similar.

Some examples of nouns include:

  • Shark
  • Tree
  • Fire
  • Library
  • Website
  • Dragon

What is a pronoun?

A pronoun is any word that refers to ownership, people, places, etc...

"Pronoun", pro-noun, literally means "instead of noun".

Some examples of pronouns include:

  • America
  • Google
  • We
  • His
  • Them
  • Thomas
