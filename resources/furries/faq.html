Why do people become furries?

People may consider themselves furries for a variety of reasons; it's important to note that not everyone's motivations or experiences are the same.

Here are some reasons why a person may consider themselves a furry:

  1. Identification — identifying with an anthropomorphic animal character may allow one to explore/express aspects of their personality, creativity, or imagination that they might not feel comfortable expressing in their everyday lives.
  2. Social comfort — for some furries, especially in cases where the furry is neurodivergent, being a furry ensures they are in a clique, and they won't need to worry as much about scrutiny from others.
  3. Creativity — some people may be (drawn to) furries because of art; anthropomorphic animal characters come up a lot in cartoons, and the choice of animal can make for fun characters designs, character traits, etc...

Can furries be any animal?

Yes.
Furries can choose any animal that they please if they feel like it represents them.

If someone's fursona is a reptile, or other hairless creature, are they still a furry?

Yes.
People whose fursonas which are animals without hair are still considered furries, and, in the case of reptiles and amphibians, are considered a subset of furries, which are called "scalies".

How do furries choose what animal they are?

It is complicated; there is no straightforward answer to why someone chooses their fursona's animal.
Some factors could include personal experiences with their animal, whether or not that animal is their "spirit animal", and the historical significance of the character that acts as their fursona*.

* — In my case, I created Katty before I considered myself a furry; however, after the character grew on me, I decided that I resonated with them a lot, and I decided to label them as my fursona.
